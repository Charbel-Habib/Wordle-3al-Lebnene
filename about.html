About Wordle 3al Lebnene

From a pandemic side-project to a daily ritual for millions — and now, a Lebanese edition.

How it all started

This project began last month during our Web Development course, when Dr. Elias Al Zaghrini challenged us to build a complete website of our choice. Whether it was an online store, a portfolio, a management system, or even a game, the only goal was to meet the project's technical requirements while creating something original. As the requirements were being explained, I, Charbel Habib, found myself scrolling through my phone and playing the New York Times game, Wordle.

A few rounds later, the idea came naturally: why not recreate the same experience with a Lebanese twist? Wordle is already popular among many Lebanese players, but its English vocabulary often makes winning depend more on luck than knowledge. Instead of unfamiliar English words, I wanted players to guess everyday Lebanese expressions, turning a familiar game into something that feels local, nostalgic, and fun.

Wordle 3al Lebnene

This version keeps the classic one-puzzle-a-day challenge while replacing the English dictionary with words every Lebanese person recognizes. The game uses the unique mix of Latin letters and numbers commonly used in WhatsApp messages and social media—to represent Arabic sounds without using the Arabic alphabet.

From popular expressions and food names to everyday slang and phrases, each puzzle celebrates the way Lebanese people communicate online. The result is a game that stays true to the original Wordle experience while giving it a personality that feels unmistakably Lebanese: simple, familiar, and guaranteed to bring a smile when the answer finally clicks.
